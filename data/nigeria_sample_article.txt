Nigeria, officially the Federal Republic of Nigeria, is a country in West Africa. It borders Niger to the north, Chad to the northeast, Cameroon to the east, and Benin to the west. Its southern coast lies on the Gulf of Guinea in the Atlantic Ocean. Nigeria is a federal republic comprising 36 states and the Federal Capital Territory, where the capital, Abuja, is located. The largest city is Lagos, one of the largest metropolitan areas in the world.
Nigeria gained independence from the United Kingdom in 1960. Nnamdi Azikiwe became the first President and Abubakar Tafawa Balewa served as the first Prime Minister. The country experienced a civil war from 1967 to 1970 when the region of Biafra attempted to secede. Nigeria returned to democratic rule in 1999 after a period of military governments.
Nigeria is a member of the African Union, the Economic Community of West African States, and the United Nations. Bola Tinubu was elected President in the 2023 general election, succeeding Muhammadu Buhari. The main political parties include the All Progressives Congress and the People's Democratic Party.
The economy of Nigeria is the largest in Africa by some measures. The country is a major producer of crude oil, and the Niger Delta region is central to petroleum production. Agriculture, telecommunications, and the film industry, known as Nollywood, are also significant. The currency of Nigeria is the naira, issued by the Central Bank of Nigeria.
Nigeria has more than 250 ethnic groups. The three largest are the Hausa in the north, the Yoruba in the southwest, and the Igbo in the southeast. English is the official language. Islam is predominant in the north and Christianity in the south.
